# Study: Balinese Pura Courtyard (Horizontal Civic Chain)

**Style:** balinese  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Sources:** Pura Besakih / Pura Ulun Danu court sequences; candi bentar → jaba tengah → jero; wantilan + petirtaan typology

## Motifs

- Split-gate threshold (candi bentar analogue) opening onto successive courts
- Processional stair and terrace ramp between court levels
- Covered gallery / colonnade wrapping the middle court
- Open pavilion (wantilan / bale) as communal sacred hall — not a vertical spine
- Ritual basin / petirtaan as court terminus

## Proportions

- Horizontal court chain preferred over meru spires
- Stair rise ~0.18–0.22 m; ramp as soft terrace connector
- Pavilion wider than tall; arcade bay ~3.0–3.4 m

## Rhythms

- Gate → stair → corridor walk → arcade → pavilion → ramp → basin
- Alternating solid thresholds and open court voids

## Structural rules

- No tower spines (no meru/pagoda as corner markers)
- Corner markers = free pillars / posts
- Sacred role = pavilion hall; monument = basin

## Ornament systems

- Stone lintel / circular gate language for thresholds
- Recessed trim on arcade and corridor greybox modules
- Soft organic growth DNA (tropical court vegetation cue)
